**Post 1: Announcement (now)**

Can good mental health 'go viral'?

On 25 March at 12pm, BLKOUT UK is opening up Critical Frequency — a proposal that uses machine learning, network science, and behavioural economics to design mental health intervention for and with Black queer men.

Not a finished product. A work in progress, shared for collective thinking.

The specificity is deliberate. Getting it right for a specific group avoids the slide into generic-serves-no-one services. The margins influence the centre.

Dr Rob Berkeley presents. Dr Ariel Breaux Torres interrogates the logic. Then your questions.

One week.

How do you design mental health intervention that transforms individuals AND communities?

When you change the evidence base, change how a community relates to treatment, and change what we actually know about what works — the system shifts whether or not it was invited to.

**Post 3: Day before (24 March)**

Tomorrow. 12pm.

When we say therapy, we don't mean Eurocentric, white-normative clinical models. We mean therapy in its broadest sense: anything that is healing for a person.

Peer support, cultural practice, community connection, and relationship-building are therapeutic interventions — not nice things that happen alongside the real clinical work.

Solidarity is built through challenge, not around it. Bring your questions.
